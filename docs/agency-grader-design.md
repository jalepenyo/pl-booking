## Problem Statement

Insurance agency owners don't know what's broken in their digital presence. They're not losing sleep over their Google review count or whether their quote form asks the right questions — they're guessing, and they don't know what they don't know. Meanwhile, PolicyLift needs a top-of-funnel lead gen machine that demonstrates value before a sales conversation ever happens.

The Agency Grader is a free, public-facing tool where an agency owner enters their business name and gets a scored audit of their digital presence — modeled after proven playbooks (Owner.com's Restaurant Grader, HubSpot's Website Grader). Every gap identified maps to a PolicyLift product. The conversion event is a demo booking, not an email capture.

## Inspiration & Proven Model

- **Owner.com Restaurant Grader** (grader.owner.com): Scans in ~30 seconds, shows competitor comparison, identifies profile errors and missing keywords, ties every gap to an Owner.com product. Their top-of-funnel lead gen machine.
- **HubSpot Website Grader**: Free tool, scores your site, captures your email, funnels you into the product. Ran for years as a primary lead gen channel.
- **Our twist**: No email gate. Google Places data already identifies the agency. Full report is free. CTA is "Book a Demo" — higher intent than email capture.

## Strategic Context — The Three-Act Narrative

PolicyLift is a lead-to-bind platform for independent P&C insurance agencies. The Grader tells the story in three acts:

**Act 1: "Get found, look legit"** (Grader scores this directly)
You can't sell insurance if nobody knows you exist. SEO, reviews, visibility, website.
- Products: SEO & AEO Optimized Website, Google Review Automation, E-Mail & SMS Marketing, Calendar Booking Tools, Digital Ads Automation

**Act 2: "Be there when they need you"** (Grader scores this directly)
When a prospect finds you, can they reach you at 10pm on a Saturday after a fender bender? Meet them where they are, how they want, when they want. They feel like a VIP without you hiring a night shift.
- Products: Voice AI (Kelly), Chat AI, 2-Way Texting, Email BYOD (coming soon)

**Act 3: "Get to the meat"** (Grader teases this — CTA only, no score)
All the context from Acts 1 and 2 flows into intake. Skip re-keying, skip phone tag. The agent goes straight to advising on risk with a quote in hand.
- Products: Digital Intake Forms, Customer Portal, Producer Workstation, Dec Page Pull, Data Enrichment, Rater & Carrier Submissions

**Key architectural decision**: The Grader scores Acts 1 and 2 (what's measurable from outside). Act 3 appears as a CTA/teaser: "You're fixing the top of funnel. Curious how to automate what happens when those leads actually come in?" This opens the door to the full platform story without making claims we can't back up with data.

## Constraints

- Accuracy is non-negotiable. A wrong score ("says I don't have a chat widget but I do") destroys credibility instantly.
- Must work for any US insurance agency findable via Google Places.
- Will live at grader.policylift.ai and also embed on agency websites PolicyLift builds.
- HubSpot integration for lead tracking (business identified via Google Places data).
- ~30-45 second scan time is acceptable (matches Owner.com's UX).
- 700MB prototype exists from January 2025 — not reusing code, but may reference for context later.

## Premises

1. The Owner.com grader playbook translates to insurance — agency owners are less digitally native, which means the "wow I didn't know that" moment hits harder, not softer.
2. Every scored check must be reliably detectable. If we can't verify it accurately, we don't score it — we say "Unable to verify" with a confidence indicator.
3. The lead capture IS the business name entry. Google Places gives us name, address, phone. No email gate. CTA is "Book a Demo."
4. The full-funnel pitch belongs in a CTA teaser, not in the scoring. We score what we can measure from outside (top of funnel). We tease the rest.
5. Credibility Signals (carrier logos, team photos, license numbers) are dropped from SCORING — but carrier logos appear as a visual during the scanning animation (60% mark) if detected. Nice to have, not scored. If detection fails, skip gracefully.
6. Competitor head-to-head comparison is deferred to V2. V1 uses benchmark comparison ("average agency in your area scores X").

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: "Reliable Three" — API-only

Score 3 categories using only API data (Google Business Profile, PageSpeed Insights): Trust & Reputation, Availability, Findability. No scraping. Ships fastest but thinner report with fewer gaps to sell against.

- Effort: S (human: ~2 weeks / CC: ~2-3 hours)
- Risk: Low
- Completeness: 7/10

### Approach B: "Full Audit" — API backbone + smart scraping (CHOSEN)

Score 4 categories: Trust & Reputation, Availability & Responsiveness, Findability, and Lead Capture & Conversion. APIs for reliable data, Firecrawl/Cloudflare for form detection, chat widget presence, mobile responsiveness. Confidence indicators for scraping uncertainty. Drops Credibility Signals entirely.

- Effort: M (human: ~4 weeks / CC: ~4-6 hours)
- Risk: Medium (scraping brittleness mitigated by confidence indicators)
- Completeness: 9/10

### Approach C: "Progressive Reveal" — Ship A, upgrade to B

Launch API-only, add scraping layer in week 2-3. Pragmatic but adds coordination overhead for minimal savings with CC.

- Effort: S then M (human: ~5 weeks total / CC: ~5-7 hours total)
- Risk: Low → Medium
- Completeness: 9/10

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B: Full Audit** — The Lead Capture & Conversion category is where most agencies fail hardest, and it maps directly to PolicyLift's forms, booking, and chat products. Three categories alone might not surface enough gaps to justify a demo — especially since many agencies have decent Google ratings. The confidence indicator ("Unable to verify") handles scraping uncertainty honestly rather than guessing wrong.

### Revised Scoring Rubric (100 points across 4 categories)

Rescaled to 100 for instant readability (54/100 is immediately meaningful; 54/80 requires math). Four categories × 25 points each. Credibility Signals (carrier logos, team photos) dropped from scoring — but carrier logos may appear as a visual in the scanning animation if detected (nice-to-have, not scored, skip gracefully if detection fails).

Categories ordered by the prospect's journey: find → trust → reach → convert.

**Category A: Visibility & SEO (25 pts)** — "Can prospects find you?"
Data source: SEMrush API + Lighthouse API + Firecrawl

| Check | Points | Detection Method | Confidence |
|-------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| Ranks for "[city] insurance" terms | 7 | SEMrush Domain Overview — check if domain ranks (top 100) for local insurance keywords (e.g., "[city] auto insurance", "[city] home insurance", "[city] insurance agent"). Pull from `domain_organic` endpoint with keyword filter. | High |
| Organic keyword count ≥ 50 | 5 | SEMrush Domain Overview — `organic_keywords` field. Agencies with <50 keywords are effectively invisible in search. | High |
| Domain Authority score ≥ 20 | 4 | SEMrush Authority Score. Most small agency sites score 10-30. Below 20 = very weak domain. | High |
| Page load under 3 seconds | 3 | Lighthouse/PageSpeed Insights API | High |
| Mobile-responsive site | 3 | Lighthouse API (mobile score ≥ 70) | High |
| Title/meta includes city + "insurance" | 3 | Firecrawl scrape — check `<title>` and `<meta description>` | High |

Maps to: SEO & AEO Optimized Website, Digital Ads Automation, Website Wizard

**Note on SEMrush API costs:** SEMrush API is usage-based (credits per request). Domain Overview + keyword data = ~2-3 API units per scan. Factor into unit economics. Consider caching SEMrush data for 7 days (SEO data changes slowly) vs. 24hrs for other checks.

**Category B: Trust & Reputation (25 pts)** — "Do they trust you?"
Data source: Google Places API

| Check | Points | Detection Method | Confidence |
|-------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| Google rating ≥ 4.5 | 8 | Places API (`rating` field) | High |
| 50+ Google reviews | 7 | Places API (`user_ratings_total`) | High |
| Review within last 30 days | 5 | Places API (`reviews[]` — check if any of 5 returned reviews has `time` within 30 days). CAVEAT: Places API returns up to 5 curated reviews, not necessarily the most recent. False negatives possible. | Low |
| Owner responds to reviews | 5 | Places API (check if `reply` field exists on ≥1 of 5 returned reviews). Same API caveat. | Low |

Maps to: Google Review Automation, Reputation Management

**Category C: Availability & Responsiveness (25 pts)** — "Can they reach you?"
Data source: Google Places API + Firecrawl

| Check | Points | Detection Method | Confidence |
|-------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| Extended hours beyond 9-5 M-F | 8 | Places API (hours) | High |
| After-hours solution detected | 7 | Firecrawl — check for known chat widget scripts OR "24/7"/"after hours" near contact terms | Medium-Low |
| Multiple contact channels (phone + chat + form) | 5 | GBP + Firecrawl | Medium |
| Chat widget present on site | 5 | Firecrawl scrape — detect known widget scripts (Intercom, Drift, Tidio, LiveChat, etc.) | Medium |

Maps to: Voice AI (Kelly), Chat AI, 2-Way Texting, Calendar Booking Tools

**Category D: Lead Capture & Conversion (25 pts)** — "Can they convert?"
Data source: Firecrawl website scraping

| Check | Points | Detection Method | Confidence |
|-------|--------|-----------------|------------|
| Quote/contact form present | 9 | Firecrawl scrape | Medium-High |
| Form asks insurance-specific questions | 8 | Firecrawl + keyword heuristic (match ≥2 insurance terms = pass, 1 = partial at 4 pts) | Medium |
| Self-service booking/calendar available | 8 | Firecrawl scrape — detect Calendly, HubSpot meetings, Acuity, or booking-related links | Medium |

Maps to: Smart Forms, Booking, Website Wizard, E-Mail & SMS Marketing

**Letter Grade Thresholds:**

| Grade | Score Range | Meaning |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| A | 80-100 | Strong digital presence — few gaps |
| B | 65-79 | Good foundation — notable gaps |
| C | 50-64 | Average — significant opportunities |
| D | 35-49 | Below average — major gaps |
| F | 0-34 | Critical — needs immediate attention |

**Scoring distribution**: Most agencies will land 40-65 (C-D range). The sweet spot: enough gaps to justify a call, not so bad they feel attacked.

**Confidence handling**: If a Medium-confidence check can't be verified (scraping fails, ambiguous result), display "Unable to verify" with a neutral icon. Do NOT score it as a failure. Show score out of 100 with asterisk: "45*/100" with note: "*3 checks could not be verified and are not counted against you." This keeps the /100 denominator consistent for comparison while being honest about detection limits.

**No-website edge case**: If the agency has no website (or site is unreachable), skip all website-dependent checks (most of A, all of C and D, parts of B). Score only Category B: Trust & Reputation (Google Places data) out of 25. Show website-dependent categories grayed-out: "We couldn't find a website for your agency. This means you're invisible to online shoppers." Strong CTA for Website Wizard. If 6+ checks return "Unable to verify," show a notice.

**Scan timeout UX (critical):**
- 0-45 sec: Normal animation with stage progression
- 45-60 sec: "Almost done — finishing up a few checks..."
- 60 sec hard limit: Show whatever results are ready. Missing checks → "Unable to verify" with neutral icon.
- Never show a blank page or infinite spinner. Always degrade to partial results.

**Google Places returns nothing:**
- Show: "We couldn't find [input] as an insurance agency. Try entering your agency's exact name as it appears on Google."
- Offer: manual entry (business name + city + state) as fallback → text search API
- This is the ONE place we lose people. Make the error state warm and actionable, not a dead end.

**No website detected:**
- Special report variant: only scores Category B (Trust & Reputation)
- Other categories grayed out with: "We couldn't find a website for your agency."
- This is a feature, not a bug — it's the strongest possible pitch for Website Wizard

### Architecture Notes

- **Data flow: Server-Sent Events (SSE).** Browser opens an SSE connection on scan start. Server fires all API checks in parallel, streams each result to the browser as it arrives. Animation layer consumes the SSE stream and populates the choreographed stages with real data. If one API is slow, others still show — no blocking.
- Scan orchestrator kicks off all checks in parallel, aggregates results
- **Cache key**: Google Place ID (stable, unique). 24hr TTL. Repeated visits show cached results with "Rescan" option.
- Firecrawl checks: 15s timeout per check. If scraping fails, mark those checks as "Unable to verify" rather than blocking the scan.
- **Total scan timeout**: 60s hard limit. If scan exceeds 45s, show whatever results are ready + "Some checks are still loading" with a poll/refresh. Never leave user staring at a stuck progress bar.
- Rate limiting on Google APIs: batch nearby agency lookups for future benchmark calculations
- The grader should be embeddable as an iframe/widget for agency websites PolicyLift builds
- **Share report**: V1 = unique URL per scan result (e.g., grader.policylift.ai/report/[place-id]). URL loads cached results. No PDF export for V1.
- **Iframed forms caveat**: Third-party form builders (JotForm, Typeform, Gravity Forms) render inside iframes that Firecrawl may not capture. If no form detected but the page has iframe elements, mark as "Unable to verify" rather than "No form found."

### Abuse Prevention & Rate Limiting

- **Per-IP rate limit**: 5 scans per hour, 20 per day. Show friendly "You've graded several agencies — come back later or book a demo to discuss your results" after limit hit.
- **Lightweight challenge**: After 3 scans from same IP in quick succession, show a simple CAPTCHA or invisible reCAPTCHA before allowing another scan. Prevents bot-driven API quota exhaustion.
- **API quota monitoring**: Alert if Google Places API usage exceeds 80% of daily quota. Degrade gracefully — serve cached results or queue scans rather than failing.

## Open Questions

1. **Embeddable widget**: Same full experience, or a simplified version for embedding on agency sites? Full embed adds iframe complexity.
2. **Re-grading frequency**: Should agencies be able to re-grade to see improvement? If so, what's the cooldown (24hr cache vs. manual rescan button)?
3. **Privacy notice**: Tool collects business data via Google Places and sends to HubSpot. Need a brief privacy disclosure on the landing page ("We use your business info to generate this report and may follow up"). CCPA/state privacy law implications TBD.
4. **Google Places lookup failure**: If autocomplete returns multiple ambiguous results or no results, show a "We couldn't find your agency" state with manual entry option (business name + city + state). Fallback to text search API.
4. **Demo booking tool**: Calendly, HubSpot meetings, or custom? Needs to pass grader context (score, gaps) to the sales rep before the call.
5. **Existing prototype**: The 700MB prototype from January — worth auditing for reusable API integration code, or clean-slate build?

### Decided: HubSpot Dedup Strategy

Match incoming grader leads against existing HubSpot contacts using **phone number** (from Google Places) as primary key, with **business name + city** as secondary match. If a match exists: UPDATE the existing contact with grader results (add "Grader Score" property, append scan timestamp to activity log). If no match: CREATE new contact. If the matched contact is already a paying customer: still update with grader data but tag as "Existing Customer — Graded" so sales doesn't re-prospect them. This is critical-path for the HubSpot integration — not an open question.

## Success Criteria

- **Lead volume**: X demo bookings per month from grader (set target after first 2 weeks of data)
- **Scan completion rate**: >90% of started scans complete successfully (no errors/timeouts)
- **Accuracy**: <5% of users report an incorrect finding in their first month
- **Time to result**: <45 seconds from "Grade My Agency" click to full results
- **Conversion funnel**: Track entry → scan complete → scroll to bottom → click "Book Demo"

### User Emotional Journey

```
STEP | USER DOES                | USER FEELS           | DESIGN SUPPORTS IT WITH
-----|--------------------------|----------------------|---------------------------
1    | Lands on grader page     | Curious, slightly    | Clean, non-threatening page. One
     |                          | skeptical            | input. No signup wall. "This is free?"
2    | Types agency name,       | Recognition —        | Google Places autocomplete shows
     | sees autocomplete        | "that's me!"         | THEIR agency with photo + address.
     |                          |                      | First trust signal: "you know who I am."
3    | Watches scan animation   | Engaged, impressed   | THEIR data appearing in real-time.
     | (30-45 sec)              | — "whoa, it found    | Each stage reveals something they
     |                          | all that about me?"  | recognize. This is the hook.
4    | Sees overall score       | Gut punch or relief  | Score donut + letter grade. Big number.
     |                          | — "54... is that     | Color coding (orange = "average") gives
     |                          | good or bad?"        | immediate emotional read.
5    | Reads category breakdown | Targeted concern     | Funnel questions personalize it:
     |                          | — "I'm failing at    | "Can people find you?" with their
     |                          | availability?"       | specific score creates ownership.
6    | Sees individual checks   | Specific urgency     | Red X on "No booking system" or
     |                          | — "I didn't know my  | "Not ranking for [city] insurance"
     |                          | site was that slow"  | — concrete, fixable, personal.
7    | Reaches CTA              | Motivated to act     | "Fix the issues" feels actionable,
     |                          | — "I need to fix     | not salesy. Demo booking = next step,
     |                          | this"                | not a commitment.
```

**The critical emotional transition is Step 4 → 5.** The score alone creates anxiety ("am I bad?"). The category breakdown transforms anxiety into agency ("HERE'S what's bad, and it's fixable"). The funnel question framing ("Can people find you?") makes it feel like a conversation, not a report card.

**Design risk:** If the score is too low (< 30), the user might feel attacked and bounce. Mitigation: always lead with what's GOOD before what's bad. "Your Google rating is excellent (5.0 ⭐) — but your online visibility is holding you back."
